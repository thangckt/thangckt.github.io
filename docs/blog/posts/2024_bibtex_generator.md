# BibTeX generator

Have you ever felt exhausted and bored dealing with manually creating BibTeX entries for your paper?

There are support tools/plugins accompanied by reference managers like Zotero, Mendeley,... that can help to automatically generate the `.bib` file. You then need to have such a reference manager, its plugins, and a collection of papers in the form of that management library installed on your computer. Even though, the BibTeX entry generated by those tools is not always correct. They normally come up with incomplete information, messy format, and abundant unnecessary fields that you still need to check and fix manually.

Sometimes, you just need to cite a paper or two, and you don't want to deal with all the hassle of the above-mentioned. Facing this situation quite often, I just want to quickly copy and paste a bib entry to the `.bib` file and that's it. Think of such a simple tool, I have looked around the Chrome extension store to see if there is any that can pick up the Bibtex while you are browsing the paper. I found some, but they do not really work.

Disappointed, I decided to create a simple tool to help me with that. It takes me a few nights to write a Chrome extension that can generate a BibTeX entry for any browsing URL with just one click. I called it the [BibTeX generator](./). It works. I finally found something helpful for my needs.

## Usage
Install the [BibTeX generator](./) extension to the Chrome browser. Then, whenever you click on the extension icon while browsing a paper or any URL, the BibTeX entry will be generated and copied to your clipboard. You can then just paste it to your `.bib` file.
