# Facing peer review

When a munuscript is sumitted to a journal, it is first evaluated by a journal editor before it is sent for peer review. The editor may evaluate the actual content of the paper, and only those papers that the editor considers potentially strong enough or novel enough progress to peer review. The other papers are rejected at this editorial 'screening stage' -- this is sometimes called **desk rejection**.

Rejection is part of the publishing process. It can happen at the screening stage (that is, before peer review) or after peer review.

## Peer review

Peer review is at the heart of scholarly publishing. Researchers who carry out peer review are called peer reviewers, journal reviewers, reviewers, or journal referees.

Peer reviewers are experts in a particular field. They are usually active researchers who have published articles in reputable journals.

A proper academic journal is one in which research papers are published only after a rigorous peer review process. The editor of such a journal is responsible for identifying appropriate peer reviewers for every paper that comes to him/her for consideration. These peer reviewers may belong to the journal's editorial board or peer review panel, or they may be people suggested by the authors at the submission stage.

In general, a scientific paper is reviewed by two or three peer reviewers.

!!! info "Types of peer review"
    - Double blind peer review: Neither the authors nor the reviewers of a paper know each other's identities
    - Single blind peer review: Authors do not know the identity of the reviewers of their paper, but the reviewers know the identity of the authors
    - Open peer review: Both the authors and the reviewers know each other's identities

!!! info "What peer reviewers do"
    1. Carefully read the paper to evaluate if it is methodologically sound and if it makes an original contribution
    2. Help the journal editor decide whether to publish the paper
    3. Try to spot errors or weaknesses in the paper
    4. Provide comments to the authors to help them improve the paper

!!! note
    - Peer review is not an easy job! Serious reviewers may spend several hours reviewing each paper that comes to them. 
    - Peer review is supposed to be a voluntary professional service. Sometimes, new journals and journals based in developing countries may find it difficult to attract or retain peer reviewers, so they might pay reviewers for their work.
    - Peer reviewers are often acknowledged or thanked by the journal in some way. For example, at the beginning of a year, some journals list the names of the reviewers who reviewed articles for that journal in the preceding year. Some journals may also offer certificates to reviewers.
    - Being a peer reviewer is a time-honoured way to serve the global scientific community.

## Dealing with reviewers' comments

- Harsh tone of reviewer: Keep calm and reply to the reviewers' comments objectively
- Problematic comments (Reviewer didn't understand a particular aspect of paper): should explain why you doesn’t agree with the problematic comment, and she should take action on the other comments.

## Presenting responses

When you get comments from peer reviewers, you may need to do some or all of the following:

- Carry out or repeat any relevant experiments or analyses suggested by the reviewers, and add the details in your paper.
- Revise any part of your paper that the reviewers have pointed out as being unclear or incomplete. Indicate the modified or new parts of your paper using Track Changes or some other method.
- Do not ignore any comments from the reviewers.
- Prepare a document to respond to the reviewers' comments and questions:
    - Present both the comments and your responses so that everything is in one document.
    - **Respond to the comments point-by-point** (the first comment, your response, the next comment, your response, and so on).
    - Point out any revisions you have made in your paper.
    - If you disagree with any comment, provide an explanation in an objective way.
    - Write clearly and politely throughout.

When you submit your revised paper to the journal, make sure you attach the document with your responses to the reviewers' comments.

!!! note
    If we need more time to revise paper, contact the editor.

## Possibilities

- Paper is rejected after a few months of review
- Paper is accepted for publication
    - Keep an eye out for editorial queries from the journal office.
    - Checking the proofs (sometimes called 'galley proofs')
        - Is the paper complete? Are all the components present?
        - Are there any typographical errors in the text or references?
        - Are the tables and figures properly placed, clear, and complete?
        - Are the author’s names and affiliations properly written without any spelling errors?
